Caffeine

How Does It Help

  • Neurotransmitters:
    Caffeine is an adenosine antagonist which influences acetylcholine, epinephrine (adrenaline), serotonin and boosts the use of dopamine. Providing the stimulant effect experienced when consuming caffeine.
  • Mood:
    Caffeine improves mood within an hour of consumption. Because it increases the density of GABA receptors, potentiates dopamine, and causes some serotonin receptors to be more responsive.

Caffeine Recommended Dosage

  • 400mg Max per day

Caffeine Side Effects

Breathing trouble Confusion Fainting
Fever Hallucination Heart palpitation
Restlessness Sweating Muscle tremors

N-Acetyl-L-tyrosine

×

How Does It Help

  • Cognitive Stress:
    NALT produces the catecholamine-triad of neurotransmitters dopamine, norepinephrine and epinephrine. Sleep deprivation and extreme stressors like heat and cold can deplete catecholamine levels. NALT restores them to preserve optimal cognition.
  • Neurotransmitters:
    NALT is a necessary precursor for dopamine, norepinephrine and epinephrine. As your dopamine levels increase, you're better able to concentrate, organize your thoughts, and stay productive.

NALT Recommended Dosage

  • N-Acetyl L-Tyrosine (NALT) suggested dosage for cognitive benefit is 350 - 500 mg twice per day.

Nalt Side Effects

At higher doses there are reports of stomach issues and migraines. NALT can increase your thyroid hormones

How Does It Help

  • Brain Optimization:
    CDP-Choline provides uridine once it enters in the brain. Uridine repairs, rebuilds and re-supplies the components needed for neuron repair. To keep signaling between neurons optimized for memory, learning, cognition and recall
  • Brain Energy:
    CDP-Choline increases Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) in brain cells. ATP is produced in your mitochondria and your main source of cellular energy.

CDP-Choline Recommended Dosage

  • Recommended CDP-Choline dosage is 250 - 500 mg per day

CDP-Choline Side Effects

Toxicity with too much choline in your body Trouble sleeping Headache

L-Theanine

×

How Does It Help

  • Brain Waves:
    L-Theanine boosts alpha brain waves (8-12Hz) promoting alert relaxation. And theta brain waves associated with creativity and relief from trauma..
  • Neuroprotection:
    L-Theanine is an agonist of NMDA receptors and can inhibit synaptic release of glutamate. Protecting your brain from over-stimulation caused by glutamate, and possible glutamate toxicity.

L-Theanine Recommended Dosage

  • Recommended dosage of L-Theanine is 100 - 400 mg once or twice per day. With a maximum of 1,200 mg per day

L-Theanine Side Effects

Decrease blood pressure

How Does It Help

  • Neuro-optimization:
    Phosphatidylserine (PS) keeps brain cells fluid and permeable. This neuroplasticity helps neurons form new connections needed for memory formation.   • Neurogenesis:
    Phosphatidylserine works in concert with the DHA in Omega-3's and keeping brain cells optimized. Prolonging neuron survival and health.

Phosphatidylserine Recommended Dosage

  • Recommended Phosphatidylserine (PS) dosage is 100 mg 3-times per day.

Insomnia Medications for Alzheimer's may interact with Phosphatidylserine Stomach upset
